Product Launch Manager

Strategic product launch expertise for technology companies вҖ” from launch planning and tiering to execution, monitoring, and retrospectives.

Philosophy

Great launches aren't about the big bang. They're about orchestrated precision that maximizes impact while minimizing risk.

The best product launches:

Tier based on impact вҖ” Not every feature deserves a keynote
Coordinate ruthlessly вҖ” Cross-functional alignment is non-negotiable
Validate before announcing вҖ” Beta programs de-risk everything
Plan for failure вҖ” Rollback plans aren't pessimism, they're professionalism
Measure what matters вҖ” Success criteria before, not after, launch
How This Skill Works

When invoked, apply the guidelines in rules/ organized by:

planning-* вҖ” Launch strategy, tiering, timelines, success criteria
coordination-* вҖ” Cross-functional alignment, RACI, stakeholder management
beta-* вҖ” Early access programs, beta cohorts, feedback loops
communication-* вҖ” Internal enablement, external messaging, launch comms
execution-* вҖ” Launch day operations, war rooms, monitoring
postlaunch-* вҖ” Retrospectives, metrics analysis, iteration

R = Responsible, A = Accountable, C = Consulted, I = Informed

Launch Timeline Template
Week -8: Launch brief, tier decision, stakeholder alignment
Week -6: Beta program begins, messaging draft
Week -4: Sales/support enablement starts, PR outreach
Week -2: Go/no-go checkpoint, final content review
Week -1: War room setup, monitoring configured, runbook complete
Day 0:   LAUNCH
Week +1: Post-launch monitoring, quick fixes
Week +2: Launch retrospective, metrics review

Success Criteria Framework
Category	Metric Type	Example
Adoption	Usage metrics	DAU, feature adoption rate, activation
Quality	Stability metrics	Error rate, P0 incidents, rollback rate
Business	Revenue metrics	Conversion, upsell, pipeline influence
Sentiment	Feedback metrics	NPS, support tickets, social sentiment
Communication Templates
Launch Brief Structure
1. Executive Summary
2. Launch Tier & Rationale
3. Target Audience
4. Key Messages (3 max)
5. Success Criteria
6. Timeline & Milestones
7. RACI & Stakeholders
8. Risks & Mitigations
9. Budget (if applicable)
10. Approval Sign-offs

Go/No-Go Checklist
в–Ў Product: Feature complete and tested
в–Ў Product: Performance benchmarks met
в–Ў Engineering: Rollback plan documented
в–Ў Engineering: Monitoring/alerts configured
в–Ў Marketing: All content published/scheduled
в–Ў Marketing: PR embargo lifted
в–Ў Sales: Enablement complete, battlecards ready
в–Ў Support: Documentation live, team trained
в–Ў Legal: Compliance review complete
в–Ў Exec: Final approval received

Anti-Patterns
Launch without tiers вҖ” Treating every release like a Tier 1 burns out teams and audiences
Big bang only вҖ” Skipping beta means learning in production
Engineering complete = launch ready вҖ” Code done вү  market ready
No rollback plan вҖ” Hope is not a strategy
Post-hoc success criteria вҖ” Defining success after launch is rationalization
Siloed launches вҖ” Marketing finds out when customers do
Launch and leave вҖ” No post-launch monitoring or iteration
Vanity launch metrics вҖ” Press mentions вү  product success
